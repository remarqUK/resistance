FX SUPPORT/RESISTANCE ZONE TRADING STRATEGY
============================================
(Compiled from all 30 NickShawnFX videos)

Overview: Mean reversion strategy trading bounces at major support and
resistance ZONES identified on the weekly/daily chart, with entries
confirmed on the 1-hour chart. Applies to forex, crypto, stocks, and
any liquid market. Designed to take 15-30 minutes per day.


================================================================================
STEP 1: IDENTIFY ZONES ON THE WEEKLY & DAILY CHART
================================================================================

- Start on the WEEKLY chart to find major zones, then use the DAILY
  chart for additional detail and confirmation. Both timeframes are used.
- Look at the last 3-6 months of price data. Do NOT use zones from
  years ago that are far from current price - they are not relevant.
- Zones should be "painfully obvious" - if you have to squint or guess,
  it is not a valid zone. Clear, consistent bounces = tradeable zone.
- A zone is a RANGE (upper and lower boundary), not a single price line.
  The zone covers the area where wicks have reversed (the rejection area).
  However, a single horizontal ray/line with breathing room in the stop
  is also acceptable as a simpler alternative.
- Look for at least 3 clear bounces/touches at the zone to confirm it
  as a major zone.
- Only draw ONE major zone above price (resistance) and ONE below price
  (support). Ignore zones further away. If no clear zone exists in one
  direction, do NOT force one - just wait.
- Avoid "hairy" zones with lots of messy spike-through wicks. Prefer
  clean, decisive bounces at the zone.
- A "break and retest" is also a valid zone: if price breaks through
  resistance and comes back to test it as support (or vice versa), this
  is a tradeable setup using the same mechanics.
- Use a clean chart: black background, all-white candles, NO indicators.

ZONE QUALITY WARNING:
- The more a zone is tested in SHORT SUCCESSION without a clean bounce,
  the LESS likely it is to hold. If price tests the same zone 4-5+ times
  in quick succession with no strong bounce away, the zone is weakening
  and a breakout becomes more likely than a bounce. Consider skipping
  the trade or trading the breakout instead.


================================================================================
STEP 2: WAIT FOR PRICE TO ENTER THE ZONE
================================================================================

- Set a TradingView price alert at approximately the MIDDLE of the zone
  (or use the horizontal ray tool -> right-click -> add alert). Your
  phone will notify you when price enters the zone.
- On Sunday, spend 15-20 minutes going through all pairs (~24-26 FX
  majors/minors) and set alerts at all major zones near current price.
- When the alert fires, switch to the 1-HOUR chart.
- Price must penetrate at least HALFWAY into the zone (target the
  midpoint) before considering an entry.
- Do NOT enter just because price is near a zone. It must be INSIDE it.
- If price has already bounced away from the zone before you see it
  (e.g. you were asleep), do NOT chase. Wait for the next setup.
- Entry must be while price is currently inside the zone. If price has
  left the zone by the time you check, skip or wait for a return.

SKIP THE ENTRY IF:
- Price is approaching the zone with very strong momentum / large candles
  in the direction of the zone (high break-through probability).
- There is a major scheduled news event (NFP, FOMC) about to occur.
- The stop loss location is ambiguous or unclear.
- Price has already moved significantly away from the zone.


================================================================================
STEP 3: WAIT FOR A REVERSAL CANDLE
================================================================================

- Once price is inside the zone (at least halfway), wait for a reversal
  candle on the 1-hour chart:
    * At a SUPPORT zone: wait for a BULLISH candle (close > open) -> LONG
    * At a RESISTANCE zone: wait for a BEARISH candle (close < open) -> SHORT
- The entry price is the close of the reversal candle.
- If price blows straight through the zone on the 1H with no
  hesitation or reversal candle, SKIP the trade entirely.
- If no alert/news issues and the zone looks valid, entering immediately
  when the alert fires is acceptable for experienced traders.
- The best winning trades start moving into profit IMMEDIATELY after
  entry. They do not fight back and forth.


================================================================================
STEP 4: SET STOP LOSS AND TAKE PROFIT
================================================================================

STOP LOSS:
- Place beyond the zone boundary AND beyond all recent wicks/extreme
  highs at the zone. The stop must clear everything.
- Give enough room that spread or liquidity spikes will not clip it
  (~0.15% buffer beyond the most extreme point is a guideline).
    * LONG at support: SL below the zone's lower boundary + all recent lows
    * SHORT at resistance: SL above the zone's upper boundary + all recent highs
- If the required stop is too tight (would be clipped by spread) or too
  wide (unreasonable risk), skip the trade.

TAKE PROFIT:
- Use a 1:1 risk-to-reward ratio. TP distance = entry-to-SL distance.
    * LONG: TP = entry + (entry - SL)
    * SHORT: TP = entry - (SL - entry)
- Do NOT use higher R:R ratios like 1.5:1 or 2:1. The strategy is
  built around 1:1 with active trade management.

WHY 1:1 (not higher R:R):
- Higher R:R = tighter stops OR further targets = lower win rate (~35-40%)
- 1:1 achieves ~50% win rate, shorter losing streaks, easier psychology
- With wider stops at 1:1, you have room to exit early and manage risk
- Higher R:R targets are often "almost hit" then reverse - 1:1 avoids this


================================================================================
STEP 5: MANAGE THE TRADE - CUT LOSSES EARLY
================================================================================

This is the MOST IMPORTANT rule. The full stop loss is only for
emergencies (~1 in 4-5 losing trades actually hits the full SL).

EARLY EXIT TRIGGERS (escalating severity):

1. SIDEWAYS ACTION (first warning): Price enters the zone, you enter,
   and price goes nowhere - just consolidates sideways. This means
   there are not enough buyers/sellers at the zone. Consider closing
   at break-even or a small loss.

2. SLOW DRIFT AGAINST YOU (second warning): Price starts making lower
   highs (for a long) or higher lows (for a short). It is slowly
   trending toward your stop. Exit for a partial loss (~0.3-0.5R).

3. ZONE BOUNDARY BREAK: A 1-hour candle CLOSES beyond the zone boundary
   (not just a wick - a wick that closes back inside is NOT a trigger).
   Exit immediately.
   * LONG: 1H candle closes below support zone's lower bound -> exit
   * SHORT: 1H candle closes above resistance zone's upper bound -> exit

4. STRUCTURE BREAK: A 1H candle closes above a recent swing high (for a
   short) or below a recent swing low (for a long). Exit for a partial loss.

5. TIME LIMIT: If the trade goes nowhere for ~2-3 days (daily chart
   setup) or ~1 week maximum, close at whatever the current P&L is.

TYPICAL EARLY EXIT RANGE: 0R (break-even) to -0.5R loss.
   - Most early exits fall between -0.2R and -0.5R
   - Average loss across all losing trades should be well below -1R
   - This asymmetry (small losses, full 1R wins) is the core edge

EXCEPTION: If there is a prior significant structural level (a clear
previous low/high) just beyond the zone, you may hold slightly longer
and only exit if THAT structural level also breaks.


================================================================================
STEP 6: MANAGE THE TRADE - HOLD WINNERS
================================================================================

- Do NOT close winning trades early. Let them run to the full TP.
- IGNORE intermediate lower-timeframe support/resistance zones between
  your entry and TP. Do not close just because price approaches a
  minor zone on the way.
- Hold approximately 80% of winning trades to the full 1:1 TP target.

EXCEPTIONS (close a winner early ~10-20% of the time):
- FRIDAY/WEEKEND: If price is 70-90% of the way to TP and the market
  is about to close for the weekend, close to avoid weekend gaps and
  spread widening.
- MAJOR NEWS: If a major scheduled news event (NFP, FOMC) is imminent
  and price is close to TP, consider closing.


================================================================================
RE-ENTRY RULES
================================================================================

- After an early exit or break-even close, you CAN re-enter the same
  trade at the same zone if the setup is still valid and price returns
  to the zone.
- After a stop-out, you CAN re-enter at the same zone if price returns.
  Consider risking a similar or larger amount on the re-entry to recoup
  the loss.
- Trading the same zone multiple times is valid - same zone, new trade.
- After completing any trade, prefer switching to a DIFFERENT pair
  rather than immediately re-trading the same one.


================================================================================
ADVANCED: COUNTER-DIRECTION (BREAKOUT) TRADES
================================================================================

When a zone has been tested many times in quick succession with:
- Multiple lower highs on 1H (at support) or higher lows (at resistance)
- No strong bounce away - just weak, failed attempts to reverse
- Daily candles closing inside the zone instead of bouncing away

...it may be appropriate to trade the BREAKDOWN/BREAKOUT instead of
the bounce (sell at support expecting it to break, or buy at resistance).

Stop loss for a breakout trade: above/below the most recent swing
high/low within the congestion area, not beyond the zone itself.


================================================================================
POSITION SIZING
================================================================================

- Risk a BASE percentage of your account per trade (default ~5%).
- Calculate position size so that if the full stop loss is hit, you
  lose exactly that percentage.
- As your account grows, position sizes grow with it (compounding).

ADJUSTMENTS:
- Risk can be varied by setup quality/conviction. Higher quality setups
  warrant higher risk; lower confidence setups warrant lower risk.
- Capital-tier scaling (reduce % as account grows):
    * Under £10K:  Higher risk (10-20%) acceptable for experienced traders
    * £10K-£100K:  Moderate risk (5-10%)
    * Over £100K:  Lower risk (2-5%) to preserve capital
- Adding to positions (scaling in) at the same zone is valid when price
  pushes deeper into the zone. Tighten stops on all entries when adding.


================================================================================
CORRELATION RISK MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

- Never hold more than 1-2 correlated pairs at the same time.
- Pairs with the same quote currency are correlated:
    * All XXX/JPY pairs (USDJPY, EURJPY, GBPJPY, etc.)
    * All XXX/USD pairs (EURUSD, GBPUSD, AUDUSD, etc.)
    * EUR/USD and GBP/USD are correlated
- When two correlated pairs both have valid setups:
    1. BEST: Pick the one with the better setup (more zone touches,
       bigger prior bounces, wider range, cleaner structure)
    2. ALTERNATIVE: Trade both but SPLIT the risk so the combined
       total = one normal unit of risk (e.g. half-size on each)
- Already in a trade on one correlated pair = skip similar setups on
  other correlated pairs unless you reduce position size.


================================================================================
WORKFLOW & PRACTICAL NOTES
================================================================================

- Trade ~24-26 FX major and minor pairs for a broad opportunity set.
- Sunday setup: 15-20 minutes reviewing all pairs, setting zone alerts.
- Daily routine: Check charts 2-4 times per day, ~15 minutes each session.
  Total chart time: 30 minutes to 1 hour per day maximum.
- Expected frequency: 3-10 setups per week, averaging ~5.
- Average trade duration: approximately 1-3 days (rarely more than a week).
- Some months you will have no trades on certain pairs - that is normal.
- Track all trades using R-multiples (e.g. +1R, -0.3R) not £ amounts,
  so P&L tracking is transferable across account sizes.
- Use a private Discord server or journal: screenshot entry + exit for
  every trade.
- Beginners: demo account for 6 months minimum (ideally 1 year) before
  live trading. Need 50-100 trades for meaningful statistical assessment.


================================================================================
PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS
================================================================================

- Win rate: ~40-50% (sufficient because losses are small, wins are full)
- Monthly expectancy: ~1-2R net profit over ~10 trades
- Annual target: ~15-30R cumulative
- Full stop-loss hits only ~1 in 4-5 losing trades (most exits are early)
- Losing streaks of 5-6+ trades in a row will happen - this is normal
- Some months will be breakeven or slightly negative - this is normal


================================================================================
SUMMARY OF THE EDGE
================================================================================

The strategy works because:
1. Zones are obvious areas where price consistently reverses
2. Entry is confirmed by price action (reversal candle inside the zone)
3. Losses are cut early (~0.3-0.5R avg) instead of taking full SL (-1R)
4. Winners are held to full 1R target
5. Average loss < average win, even with a modest win rate
6. 1:1 R:R gives ~50% win rate and short losing streaks
7. Correlation management prevents overexposure

With ~50% win rate, winners pay +1R and losers cost only ~0.3-0.5R on
average, producing consistent positive expectancy over time.


================================================================================
QUICK REFERENCE
================================================================================

Timeframes:     Weekly/Daily (zones) + 1-Hour (entries)
Entry:          Reversal candle inside zone, at least halfway in
Stop Loss:      Beyond zone boundary + all recent wicks + breathing room
Take Profit:    1:1 risk-to-reward
Early Exit:     Sideways action, drift against, zone break, or time limit
Hold Winners:   To full TP (exceptions: Friday close, major news)
Re-entry:       Valid at same zone after early exit or stop-out
Position Size:  Base ~5% of account (adjust by conviction & capital tier)
Correlation:    Max 1-2 correlated pairs at once, split risk if both taken
Min Touches:    Zone must have 3+ clean bounces to qualify
Chart Setup:    Clean chart, no indicators, white candles on black
Workflow:       Alerts on zones, check charts 2-4x/day, ~15 min each
